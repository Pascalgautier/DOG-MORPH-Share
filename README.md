DOG MORPH
DOG MORPH is a hybrid quadruped robotics platform designed for broadcast applications and advanced mobility research. Its core value lies in obstacle traversal, including stair climbing and ramp navigation up to 45 degrees, while maintaining stable operation for camera payloads.

The project starts with teleoperation, then evolves toward semi-autonomous behavior, and ultimately full autonomy. In parallel, a digital twin is being developed in NVIDIA Omniverse and Isaac Lab to simulate locomotion, carving behavior, obstacle negotiation, camera dynamics, optics, and 3-axis gimbal stabilization with systems such as the Shotover G1 or DJI Ronin 2.

DOG MORPH aims to combine mobility, precision, and cinematic camera control in a single robotic platform.

Roadmap
The project roadmap is structured in three stages.

Teleoperation
The first version of DOG MORPH is operated remotely by a human pilot for direct control and validation in real environments.

Semi-autonomous operation
The second phase introduces assisted behaviors such as navigation support, trajectory handling, and partial autonomy for complex movement tasks.

Full autonomy
The final stage aims to enable the robot to navigate, adapt, and execute mission profiles autonomously while carrying a stabilized camera payload.

In parallel, the digital twin will be refined in simulation to accelerate testing, tuning, and control development.

Technical Stack
DOG MORPH combines robotics, simulation, and broadcast technologies across both physical and virtual environments.

Robotics platform: Quadruped hybrid locomotion system.

Simulation: NVIDIA Omniverse, Isaac Lab.

Control mode: Teleoperation, then semi-autonomous and autonomous development.

Payload: Broadcast camera system with optics and 3-axis gimbal stabilization.

Use case: Obstacle traversal, stair climbing, ramp navigation, and cinematic camera motion.

Digital twin: Used for locomotion testing, behavior validation, and motion tuning.
