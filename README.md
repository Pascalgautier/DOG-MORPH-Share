DOG MORPH
DOG MORPH is a hybrid quadruped robotics platform designed for broadcast applications and advanced mobility research. Its core value lies in obstacle traversal, including stair climbing and ramp navigation up to 45 degrees, while maintaining stable operation for camera payloads.

The project starts with teleoperation, then evolves toward semi-autonomous behavior, and ultimately full autonomy. In parallel, a digital twin is being developed in NVIDIA Omniverse and Isaac Lab to simulate locomotion, carving behavior, obstacle negotiation, camera dynamics, optics, and 3-axis gimbal stabilization with systems such as the Shotover G1 or DJI Ronin 2.

DOG MORPH aims to combine mobility, precision, and cinematic camera control in a single robotic platform.
